Who can live in Ingersoll Senior Residences?

by Allie Weintraub and K. Dominic McKenzie

George Phipps is estranged from the family he says do not know he is gay and living with HIV. But, as an aging member of the New York City population, he has found comfort living in a building where there are others like him, with the medical services he needs to enjoy the latter years of his life.

“Quote about having support in house” said Phipps, a resident at the Keith D. Cylar House Residence & Health Center, a service of Housing Works. Founded in 1990, Housing Works is a New York City nonprofit that provides housing for people living with HIV.

He is now in search of a comfortable place he can call his permanent home.

A recent report by AARP, the nation’s largest nonprofit dedicated to healthy livelihoods for the elderly in the United States — found that as many as 91 percent of LGBT seniors are interested in LGBT-friendly housing developments with in-house support and social services for older adults.

In New York City, No LGBT senior specific residence exists.

As the city’s population continues to age, those who identify as LGBT among the aging are interested in housing that features on-site health services, and like Phipps, care for those living with HIV.

Services and Advocacy for GLBT Elders (SAGE) has partnered with the New York City Housing Authority (NYCHA) to build two LGBT-friendly developments, the Crotona Senior Residences in the Bronx and Ingersoll Senior Residences in Brooklyn.

In order to qualify for the Brooklyn development, residents must make 60 percent or less than the area median income (AMI). For Crotona in the Bronx, 50 percent or less of the AMI will help determine eligibility.
